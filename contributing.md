# Contributing to Awesome Attribution

Thanks for wanting to contribute. This list is intentionally opinionated about *how* things are listed, not *what* is listed — we include every serious project and vendor, but we ask that descriptions stay flat and honest.

## The rules

### 1. One entry per PR

Bundling multiple additions makes review harder and slower. Open a separate PR for each new entry.

### 2. Alphabetical order within every section

No ranking. Entries are sorted alphabetically by name, ignoring "The" and leading articles. If you want your tool at the top, you'll need a name that starts with an early letter.

### 3. Honest one-line descriptions

Descriptions must be:

- **One sentence.** No paragraphs, no bullet lists inside the entry.
- **Flat.** No superlatives ("best", "leading", "industry-standard", "next-generation", "revolutionary").
- **Specific.** Say what the tool does and who it's for, not how wonderful it is.
- **Un-promotional.** If your description reads like homepage copy, rewrite it. If a reviewer can't distinguish your entry from a press release, it will be edited or rejected.

Good: "B2B revenue attribution platform focused on buyer journey and sales-pipeline contribution."

Bad: "The leading B2B attribution platform trusted by the world's fastest-growing companies to unlock revenue intelligence."

### 4. No "best of" or ranking language

This list does not rank, recommend, or endorse. Every entry is listed as an option, not as a winner. Comparative language ("unlike X") belongs on your own blog, not in this list.

### 5. Quality bar

**Commercial tools:**
- Must have a live homepage and working product
- Must be actually available for sale (not pre-launch or zombie)
- Must be attribution, measurement, or adjacent — no generic CRM, CDP, or martech unless they materially contribute to the measurement problem

**Open-source projects:**
- Must have commits in the last 12 months
- Must have a README that explains what it does
- Must be genuinely useful, not a class project or abandoned prototype

**Research papers and blogs:**
- Must be linkable to a stable URL
- Papers should prefer the canonical publication over a blog summary
- Blogs must have new writing in the last 12 months

### 6. Self-listing is allowed — under the same bar

If you work on an attribution tool, you can submit it. You get no special treatment and no extra leniency. If anything, we'll be more critical of self-submissions because they're more likely to drift into promotional language.

Declare the relationship in your PR description: "I work on X" or "I maintain Y." This keeps the process honest.

### 7. No affiliates, no sponsored placements, no "featured" entries

This list has no commercial relationships and accepts no payment for inclusion or positioning. Ever.

### 8. Removals happen

Entries are removed without ceremony if:

- The product is sunset, the company is dissolved, or the homepage is dead
- The open-source project has been unmaintained for 24+ months and has no successor
- The description turns out to be misleading or the entry was added in bad faith
- The category has moved on and the entry is no longer relevant

Removals are not editorial judgments on the product — they're maintenance.

## PR format

Title: `Add [name]` or `Update [name]` or `Remove [name]`

Description:

```
Category: <section name>
Homepage: <url>
Relationship (if any): <e.g. "I work on this project" or "none">

Proposed entry:
- [Name](https://...) — One-sentence flat description.
```

## What happens after you open a PR

1. A maintainer reviews within a week (usually sooner).
2. If the entry needs edits (tone, alphabetical placement, link), we'll comment or push a small commit.
3. Merged PRs get a thank-you, not a release announcement. That's by design — the list is the news.
